A full list of genetic combinations.

Basic

  • Chestnut: A/A or A/a
  • Bay: any combination of A/_ and E/_
  • Black: E/E
  • Grey: G/g (any base coat can grey out as long as it has G)

White Markings

Some genes appear more on certain breeds...

  • Tobiano: To/_
  • Sabino: Sb/_, or SB1/_
  • Rabicano: Rb/_
  • Leopard: Lp/_
  • Varnish: PATN1/_
  • Overo: O/o

    • Homozygous O/O causes Lethal White Syndrome and cannot be bred succesfully.

  • Splash White: SW1-SW8
  • Dominant White: W1-W30+

DILUTIONS

Cream Dilutions

  • Palomino: A/_, Cr/n
  • Buckskin: E/_, A/_, Cr/n
  • Smokey Black: E/_, Cr/n
  • Cremello: A/_, Cr/Cr
  • Perlino: A/_, E_, Cr/Cr
  • Smokey Cream: E/_, Cr/Cr

Roan Dilutions

  • Red Roan: A_, Rn/_
  • Bay Roan: E/_, A/_, Rn/_
  • Blue Roan: E/_, Rn/_

Champagne Dilutions

  • Classic Champagne: E/_, Ch/_
  • Gold Champagne: A_, Ch/_
  • Amber Champagne: E/_, A/_, Ch/_

Pearl Dilutions

Pearl must be homozygous, Prl/Prl, to show on any coat.

  • Pearl: A/_, Prl/Prl
  • Black Pearl: E/_, Prl/Prl
  • Bay Pearl: E/_, A/_, Prl/Prl

Pearl Dilutions

Silver can be carried by chestnut coats, but does not show.

  • Silver Dapple: E/_, Z/_
  • Silver Bay: E/_, A/_, Z/_

Dun Dilutions

  • Red Dun: A/_, Rn/_
  • Grullo: E/_, Rn/_
  • Bay Dun: E/_, A/_, Rn/_

Rare Genes

  • Brindle: BR1/_

    • Brindle appears in the XY chromosomes. Therefore males cannot be homozygous BR1, because they only have one X chromosome. Females can be BR1/BR1, as they have two XX chromosomes.

  • Flaxen: A/_, f/f

    • Flaxen lightens the mane on a chestnut coat, but is recessive and difficult to pass along.

  • Chimera: ?

    • Chimera is the fusion of two embryos in the womb, creating abnormal patternings. It cannot be bred for.
